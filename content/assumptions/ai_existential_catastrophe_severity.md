## How severe should AI-caused existential catastrophe be modeled per affected person per year?

This document estimates the average welfare shortfall, in QALY-equivalents, conditional on an **AI-caused existential catastrophe** occurring.

The category page models a broader event than extinction alone. It includes:

- literal human extinction
- irreversible human disempowerment by AI systems
- stable totalitarian lock-in or other permanent global conditions that drastically curtail humanity's future

**Summary:** A reasonable central estimate is that such a catastrophe reduces welfare by about **0.75 QALY-equivalents per affected person per year**, with a plausible range of **0.5–0.9**. Given the current decomposition on the probability page, that overall average can be thought of as roughly **0.9** for the extinction component and about **0.45** for the non-extinction component, weighted by the site's rough `8% + 4%` split between those pathways.

---

## 1. Extinction is near the top of the range

For ordinary human life, a full life-year is not worth a perfect 1.0 QALY on average. Healthy-life-expectancy data suggest something closer to the high `0.8`s or about `0.9` as a rough all-things-considered benchmark. ([WHO](https://www.who.int/data/gho/data/themes/mortality-and-global-health-estimates/ghe-life-expectancy-and-healthy-life-expectancy), [Our World in Data](https://ourworldindata.org/life-expectancy))

So if the catastrophe is literal extinction, the welfare loss per affected person-year is near the top of the range, around **0.9**.

That WHO-style anchor applies most directly to **death** or full loss of ordinary human life-years. It should not simply be copied over to permanent non-extinction catastrophes, because those involve people remaining alive under drastically worse civilizational conditions rather than disappearing entirely.

---

## 2. Non-extinction catastrophes: about 0.45 for the central component

The harder question is how to treat **irreversible disempowerment** and **stable totalitarian lock-in**.

These are not just "bad policy" or "ordinary authoritarianism." The modeled event is much narrower and more severe: it is a permanent global condition in which humanity loses the ability to choose its own future. That is why it belongs under the existential-risk umbrella in the first place. ([Bostrom 2002](https://nickbostrom.com/existential/risks))

Recent work supports treating those outcomes as severe in their own right:

- [Dung 2025](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00146-024-01930-2) argues that AI could permanently disempower humanity even when extinction is not the immediate mechanism.
- [Kulveit et al. 2025](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.16946) argue that gradual AI development could create an effectively irreversible loss of human influence over crucial societal systems.
- [Tokson 2025](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5182213) and [Feldstein 2019](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3314162) show how AI can facilitate surveillance, repression, and concentration of power, making lock-in more technologically plausible.

If a future is globally locked in under AI or AI-enabled institutions, people may still be alive, but much of what gives human civilization its welfare value is gone or sharply reduced:

- agency and self-direction
- political freedom and pluralism
- the ability to revise institutions and correct course
- large parts of culture, ambition, and collective self-authorship

So the welfare loss should be **well below zero-to-minor-harm territory**. It should remain very large.

Given the current probability decomposition on the category page, the implied central severity of the **non-extinction** component is about **0.45** rather than 0.75 or 0.9:

- overall catastrophe severity: about `0.75`
- extinction component: about `0.9`
- extinction share of catastrophe probability: about `8 / 12`
- non-extinction share of catastrophe probability: about `4 / 12`

Solving for the non-extinction component gives roughly:

- `(0.08 x 0.9 + 0.04 x x) / 0.12 = 0.75`
- `x ≈ 0.45`

That may sound lower than a first-pass intuition, but it still represents an enormous permanent welfare loss. A `0.45` shortfall means nearly half of the value of ordinary human life-years is gone for as long as the lock-in or disempowerment lasts. That is compatible with people still having some relationships, pleasures, and projects while losing much of the agency, pluralism, political freedom, and civilizational self-direction that make human futures valuable.

---

## 3. Why the overall weighted average is 0.75

Using **0.9** for the whole event class would effectively treat permanent lock-in as almost identical to extinction in welfare terms. That is too strong. In many non-extinction catastrophes, people still have relationships, pleasures, projects, and some residual freedom.

Using **0.45** for the whole event class would swing too far the other way. It would treat the average catastrophe in this broader class as though it looked like the non-extinction component all the time, ignoring that the category also gives substantial weight to literal extinction.

So **0.75** is a reasonable middle position:

- high enough to reflect that these are existential catastrophes, not ordinary social harms
- lower than extinction, because a material share of the modeled probability mass is in non-extinction futures where people remain alive
- consistent with the current `8% extinction + 4% non-extinction` decomposition used on the probability page

### Plausible range: 0.5–0.9

- **0.5** fits views where non-extinction pathways dominate more of the catastrophe class and preserve substantial everyday welfare even while permanently curtailing humanity's broader potential.
- **0.9** fits views where the main relevant mass is extinction or where permanent lock-in is treated as nearly as bad as extinction over the modeled horizon.

This range applies to the **overall weighted-average catastrophe class**, not specifically to the non-extinction component by itself.

---

## 4. Conclusion

The best single estimate is:

- **Severity:** about **0.75 QALY-equivalents lost per affected person per year**
- **Plausible range:** about **0.5–0.9**

This is the right order of magnitude once AI existential catastrophe is understood to include both extinction and permanent non-extinction outcomes such as irreversible disempowerment and totalitarian lock-in.
