# Internal Notes

Living status / role: verified from current Freedom Together Foundation materials and the 2024 Form 990-PF, which lists Barbara Picower as President Emerita/Chair from 2024. Freedom Together says The JPB Foundation was founded by Barbara Picower in 2012, renamed Freedom Together Foundation in 2024, and made 3,900 grants totaling about $2.7B from 2012 through 2023.

Birth date: exact birth date not found in reliable public sources. Freedom Together's Beyond the Grant report says Barbara "recently turned 80" in an early-2023 conversation, implying a birth year around 1943, but this file intentionally omits birthDate rather than inventing a specific date.

Net worth: no current Forbes/Bloomberg-style personal net-worth estimate found. The value here uses Freedom Together Foundation's FY2024 Form 990-PF net assets ($3.362B) as a proxy for controlled philanthropic capital, not as a clean estimate of Barbara Picower's private wealth.

Vehicle accounting: recorded rows use annual Form 990-PF "Contributions Received" into The JPB Foundation / Freedom Together Foundation (EIN 90-0747216). These are counted when money enters Barbara Picower's personal charitable vehicle and JPB/Freedom Together downstream grants are intentionally not itemized as separate Barbara gifts. The FY2024 XML Schedule B identifies the $1.2M contributor as Estate of Jeffry M Picower. Earlier Schedule B contributor schedules were not all individually inspected, but public foundation history and filings tie the foundation to Barbara Picower and the remaining Jeffry Picower estate assets after the Madoff settlement. Jeffry Picower died in 2009, so there is no Jeffry Picower donor file; estate/household/foundation inflows are credited to Barbara as the living founder, chair, and estate-side philanthropic decision-maker.

Excluded candidates: the roughly $7.2B Picower/Madoff settlement is victim restitution, not charitable giving. The former Picower Foundation's downstream grants, including reported support for MIT, Human Rights First, and the New York Public Library, are excluded because they are foundation grants out and pre-2011 donor-to-foundation inflows were not observable in parsed public data; counting those grants would also risk mixing Jeffry Picower's lifetime giving with Barbara's later estate-directed vehicle. Barbara's anecdotal first $25 gift to SNCC is excluded as de minimis and not independently dateable.
