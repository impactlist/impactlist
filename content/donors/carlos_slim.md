# Internal Notes

- Verified living as of June 2026 (born 1940): on Forbes' 2026 World's Billionaires list (#16) and held his annual press conference in 2026.
- `netWorth`: US$125B per the Forbes 2026 World's Billionaires list (rank #16), as reported by Mexico News Daily (March 2026); Bloomberg had him at $99.1B in July 2025.
- Solo donor file: his wife Soumaya Domit died in 1999; all recorded gifts are credited 1.0 to him.
- **Personal-vs-corporate boundary (the key filter for this donor).** Fundación Telmex Telcel is a corporate foundation funded by Telmex/Telcel (América Móvil), not by Slim personally — its entire ~US$4B asset base (2007 announcement) and all of its programs (Copa Telmex sports, scholarships, disaster aid, the $1M+ Telmex gave the Clinton Foundation) are deliberately excluded. No personal contribution by Slim into Fundación Telmex is documented anywhere we could find.
- **Pledge-vs-transfer.** His 2007 pledge to grow his foundations' endowments from US$4B to US$8B over four years (WHO Bulletin, Aug 2007) and the various "$10B" headlines are pledges — no rows. The recorded rows are the two US$2B dividend transfers (2006 and 2010) into Fundación Carlos Slim that Forbes reported in May 2011 when it ranked him fifth among the world's biggest givers with US$4B lifetime giving. Mexican foundations file no public 990 equivalent; the foundation's SAT tax-transparency assets of roughly MXN 60–70B (~US$3.5–4B, per the figures collected on Spanish Wikipedia) are consistent with these two transfers and with no later inflows of similar scale. His SC 13D/A filings on Telmex/América Móvil (2006–2007) show Fundación Carso actively investing alongside the family during this window, corroborating that real money was moving into the foundation.
- **Instituto Carlos Slim de la Salud US$500M endowment (May 2007): excluded** as a separate row. The WHO Bulletin (Braine, Aug 2007) frames it inside the same endowment-growth program as the $4B pledge; Forbes' vetted lifetime figure of $4B ($2B + $2B) would otherwise imply $4.5B; and the institute (whose parent is Fundación Carlos Slim) was endowed with América Móvil/IDEAL shares while Fundación Carso was itself accumulating América Móvil shares in Feb–Apr 2007 — a plausible inter-vehicle routing. Conservative call: treat the institute's capitalization as inside the recorded $4B.
- **Excluded as foundation grants OUT** (money already counted entering Fundación Carlos Slim; captured instead in that recipient's category fractions): $100M to the Global Polio Eradication Initiative (2013), $50M to Salud Mesoamérica 2015 (2010, with Gates and Spain via the IDB), $65M (2010) + $74M (2013) to the Broad Institute for SIGMA, the $100M WWF conservation alliance (2009), $25M for CIMMYT with the Gates Foundation (2013), ~$105M (MXN 1,978M) for 2017 earthquake reconstruction, MXN 1,000M COVID-19 response (2020), $100M for cataract surgeries in Peru, and the $20M Haiti SME fund with Clinton/Giustra (2010).
- Other exclusions: the 2007 Clinton Giustra Sustainable Growth Initiative $100M pledge (no documented personal transfers); pre-2006 seeding of Fundación Carso (est. 1986, unobservable — no public-registry filings); Museo Soumaya and Centro Histórico spending (foundation/corporate money); Metro Line 12 reconstruction (Grupo Carso liability); F1 sponsorships (corporate marketing).
- He declined the Giving Pledge and is publicly skeptical of conventional philanthropy ("Foundations do not solve poverty"), which is consistent with finding no direct personal gifts outside his own vehicles.
