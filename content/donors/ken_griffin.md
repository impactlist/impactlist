# Internal Notes

Giving-vehicle treatment (decided while building this file):

- Griffin's primary giving vehicle is the **Kenneth C. Griffin Charitable Fund**, an opaque donor-advised fund / charitable fund with no public filing that exposes inflows. The file now follows the record-money-once rule: downstream grants from that vehicle or from the Griffin Catalyst platform are not itemized as donor rows. Instead, `content/donations/ken_griffin.md` has one aggregate floor row to `kenneth-c-griffin-charitable-fund`, dated 2026-02-19, built from the sum of the 55 source-backed public grant announcements that were previously itemized. The recipient file uses those downstream grants only to estimate category fractions.
- The **Kenneth and Anne Griffin Foundation** (EIN 36-4747915) operated roughly 2009-2014 as a near-zero-asset pass-through and wound down after the 2015 divorce. ProPublica shows FY2013 contributions received of $6.915M, so that is recorded as an inflow to the foundation recipient. The two large pre-2013 public grants whose inflows are not visible ($10M UChicago/Harvard education study and $16M Children's Memorial/Lurie hospital gift) are represented by one $26M aggregate floor row to the same foundation recipient, rather than by downstream charity rows.
- The **Citadel Group Foundation** (EIN 36-4482467) is the firm's small employee/partner vehicle (~$0.3-2M/yr revenue) and is excluded entirely, as are donations attributed to Citadel or Citadel Securities (e.g., $7.5M for Hubei COVID relief in Feb 2020 and ~$2.5M from "Griffin and his partners" for Chicago schools/food in Mar 2020).
- Itemized rows kept outside the vehicle aggregate are restricted to sources that identify the individual donor(s), not the opaque charitable fund or Griffin Catalyst, as the gift source: Art Institute of Chicago $19M, Fourth Presbyterian $11.5M, Harvard $150M, Harvard $300M, the Harvard residual ~$50M implied by Harvard's official ">$500M over four decades" statement, and the Dec 2023 $400M MSK joint gift with David Geffen (credited 50/50 and recorded once).
- The previous Oct 2025 Northwestern Lake Forest Hospital naming estimate is excluded from the aggregate because the amount is undisclosed ("among the largest in the health system's history") and the prior $50M was speculative, not a documented floor.
- Excluded by policy: all political giving (Griffin is one of the largest US Republican donors, including major ballot-measure and campaign spending); his $43.2M purchase of a first-edition U.S. Constitution (2021) and related document loans (acquisitions/loans, not donations); the Apex stegosaurus purchase/loan to AMNH; the contingent extra $5M Villa Serena endowment pledged alongside the Nov 2025 Vizcaya gift; Michael Sacks's $2.5M share of the 2022 Crime Lab academies funding; Schmidt Futures' and the Bezos Family Foundation's co-funder shares; and the prior speculative Northwestern Lake Forest estimate.
- Pre-2009 gifts made jointly with then-wife Anne Dias Griffin (Art Institute 2006, Fourth Presbyterian, the 2009 education study) are credited 1.0 to Ken: the philanthropy was publicly identified primarily with him, the funds derived from Citadel earnings, and Anne has no independent public wealth profile from which to source a required donor netWorth.
