# Internal Notes

Michael Milken is living as of this 2026-06-12 pass. Wikipedia lists his birth date and current age, and Los Angeles Business Journal profiled him in its 2025 Wealthiest Angelenos list.

Net worth uses Los Angeles Business Journal's October 2025 Wealthiest Angelenos estimate of $7.1 billion. Forbes profile access was blocked during this pass; Wikipedia's Forbes-derived figure was stale at $6 billion as of 2022.

Lori Milken is not given a separate donor file in this pass. The Michael and Lori Milken Family Foundation rows are credited fully to Michael, with Lori named in row notes, consistent with the add-donor spouse default.

## Giving structure (why PCF and the Milken Institute are not separate rows)

Milken's giving runs through two private foundations, both recorded as contribution-inflow rows: the Milken Family Foundation (EIN 95-4073646, co-founded 1982 with his brother Lowell, hence the 50% Michael share on its rows) and the newer Michael and Lori Milken Family Foundation (EIN 82-1927087, tax-exempt 2018, fully his). The Prostate Cancer Foundation (founded by Milken in 1993) and the Milken Institute are **downstream grantees of these foundations**, not separate giving — the Prostate Cancer Foundation is documented as one of the Milken Family Foundation's largest grant recipients. Recording PCF or the Institute as their own rows would double-count money already captured by the foundation inflows, so they are intentionally excluded as rows.

## Excluded candidates

- **Funds Milken *raised* for the Prostate Cancer Foundation** (he is famous for raising "hundreds of millions" from the public, and personally underwrites PCF's operating costs so ~100% of outside gifts reach research): excluded — only his own contributions are credited, not money solicited from third parties. Public sources conflate raised, granted, and personal giving into a "$1B+ medical giving" figure; the recorded total deliberately counts only verifiable contributions into his own vehicles.
- **Direct personal underwriting of PCF administrative/operating costs**, to the extent any bypassed the family foundations: a likely conservative under-count, but no public figure separates it from foundation-routed giving, so no speculative row is added.
- **Grants paid out by either Milken foundation** (to PCF, the Milken Institute, Milken Educator Awards, Simon Wiesenthal Center, etc.): excluded as downstream foundation outflows; the inflow rows capture this money.
- **The Milken Family Foundation rows cover only fiscal years with observable nonzero contributions** in parsed filings (FY2011, FY2013, FY2020, FY2022); contributions there trailed off as giving shifted to the 2018 Michael and Lori vehicle.
