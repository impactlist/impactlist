# Notes

Schwarzman is living as of 2026 and remains chairman and CEO of Blackstone. Major public gifts after the Stephen A. Schwarzman Foundation became active are treated carefully: when a gift appears to be a grant paid by his own foundation, this file records the contribution into the foundation instead of the foundation's later grant to the operating recipient.

# Internal Notes

Flagship pledges — MIT (~$350M, announced October 2018) and Oxford (£150M, announced June 2019): could not confirm routing; pledges not recorded as rows pending evidence of payment. ProPublica's parsed 990-PF pages for the Stephen A. Schwarzman Foundation show cumulative charitable disbursements of only ~$413M across FY2018–FY2024 with total assets never above ~$125M, so the two pledges (~$540M combined) cannot both have been fully paid through the foundation in that window; some installments may flow through its annual disbursements ($42M–$82M/yr from FY2019), but the parsed pages do not expose grantee lists and the per-filing XML was not retrievable in this session. Direct personal payment was not evidenced either, so no MIT/Oxford rows exist.

Giving vehicles identified in research:

- Stephen A. Schwarzman Foundation, EIN 47-4634539, tax-exempt September 2015.
- Stephen A. Schwarzman Education Foundation, EIN 45-4757735, tax-exempt September 2013.

The Stephen A. Schwarzman Education Foundation later received large contributions from Tsinghua University Education Foundation and the Stephen A. Schwarzman Scholars Trust, so annual Education Foundation contribution totals after the initial Schwarzman Scholars commitment are not treated as fully Schwarzman-funded.

## Excluded candidates

- Political giving (campaign committees, super PACs, 501(c)(4) advocacy — Schwarzman has been one of the largest Republican mega-donors): excluded by policy; political contributions are deliberately out of scope.
- Grants paid out by the Stephen A. Schwarzman Foundation and the Education Foundation (e.g. later Inner-City Scholarship Fund grants): excluded as downstream foundation outflows; the inflow rows capture this money.
