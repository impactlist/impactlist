# Internal Notes

Ballmer Group is Steve and Connie Ballmer's grantmaking vehicle (an LLC whose grants flow through their Goldman Sachs Philanthropy Fund donor-advised fund); this recipient also stands in for Rainier Climate Group, the climate vehicle the family added in August 2024. Fractions approximate the share of grant dollars across their ~$7B of cumulative grantmaking (2015 – early 2026), not the count of programs:

- The Chronicle of Philanthropy's ten-year retrospective (https://www.philanthropy.com/news/power-couple-giving-the-10-year-journey-of-steve-and-connie-ballmer/) reports economic mobility as roughly 80% of the active portfolio, in a "cradle-to-career" mix: early-childhood and K-12 education, college access, career/workforce, plus place-based community investment, housing/homelessness, and criminal-justice reform. That bucket is decomposed here as education 0.40, local-community 0.18 (place-based economic-mobility infrastructure such as StriveTogether networks, Blue Meridian Partners' $350M 2024–2029 commitment, YMCA of Greater Seattle), housing 0.06, and social-justice 0.05 (criminal-justice grantees such as The Bail Project and Equal Justice Initiative, plus child welfare).
- climate-change 0.15: just over $1B of climate giving 2022–2024 (Ballmer Group commitments of $217M in Oct 2022 and $431M in Nov 2023, then Rainier Climate Group), per Inside Philanthropy and the Chronicle.
- health-medicine 0.10: children's behavioral/mental health, dominated by the $425M Ballmer Institute for Children's Behavioral Health at the University of Oregon (2022) and $110M for the LA youth mental-health workforce (2026).
- disaster-relief 0.03: $65M of 2025 LA wildfire relief (direct gift plus FireAid match) and earlier COVID-19 relief (e.g. All In Washington).
- institutions 0.03: civic-data work in the USAFacts orbit and grants like New America's Eviction Data Response Network.
