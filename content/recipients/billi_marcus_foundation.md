# Internal Notes

Category split is based mainly on the FY2024 Form 990-PF XML grant schedule for the Billi Marcus Foundation, which listed Billi Marcus as chairperson and $2.26M in charitable disbursements. The largest grants were Jewish/religious and Israel-related education/service recipients, including Torah Academy of Boca Raton ($250k), American Friends of Magen David Adom ($150k), Nefesh B'Nefesh ($100k), Jewish Education Center of South Florida ($99.2k), Marcus Jewish Community Center ($80k), Jewish Association for Residential Care ($52.5k), Jewish federations, Chabad, Jewish film/life festivals, and related schools. Health-medicine includes United Hatzalah, Israel Cancer Association, hospice, ovarian cancer, Shepherd Center, Emory Winship, Boca Raton Regional Hospital, and disease foundations. Local-community includes food banks, adoption/family services, veterans/public-safety charities, camps, housing, and local human services. Education covers Jewish and secular schools/camps where the grant purpose is school/camp operations rather than worship. Small allocations reflect human-rights, arts/culture, animal welfare, and global-development/global-health-adjacent grantees such as Lantos Foundation, film/music/museum recipients, animal rescue/guide dog groups, FINCA, Mercy Ships, Doctors Without Borders, and Partners in Health.
