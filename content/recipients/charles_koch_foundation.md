# Internal Notes

Split is an approximate portfolio model for the foundation, not a grant-by-grant reconstruction. ProPublica classifies the foundation under NTEE B82, scholarships/student financial aid, and recent filings list many higher-education program staff and university-investment roles. TIME summarized Koch-controlled 2017 charitable giving as 95% educational programs and community groups addressing poverty, including free-speech, HBCU debate, and criminal-justice/recidivism research. The split therefore puts most weight on education, with an institutions share for public-policy/free-society research and a social-justice share for criminal-justice reform and similar community work. Sources: https://projects.propublica.org/nonprofits/organizations/480918408 and https://time.com/5413786/charles-koch-charitable-giving/
