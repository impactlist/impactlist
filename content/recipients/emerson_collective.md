# Internal Notes

This recipient stands in for Laurene Powell Jobs' philanthropic giving through Emerson Collective LLC and Waverley Street Foundation / Emerson Collective Foundation under the LLC floor-method row in `content/donations/laurene_powell_jobs.md`.

The category split is based on the documented floor amount, not on every opaque grant Emerson may have made:

- climate-change 0.85: the recorded floor is dominated by Waverley Street Foundation, Powell Jobs' climate philanthropy. ProPublica Form 990-PF summaries show Waverley charitable disbursements of $84,751,594 in 2020, $124,899,654 in 2021, $168,206,559 in 2022, $237,463,886 in 2023, and $306,163,099 in 2024. AP also reported $185M into a donor-advised fund during 2017-2019 and described Waverley as the vehicle for Powell Jobs' climate initiatives: https://apnews.com/article/climate-change-business-environment-and-nature-philanthropy-apple-inc-70233d6b5dd94ad989e885f0db426860
- education 0.10: the separate large documented non-Waverley item is XQ, funded by Emerson Collective; Time reported $100M of XQ Super School awards, 10 schools at $10M each, over five years: https://time.com/4495705/xq-super-school-grant-american-high-school/
- social-justice 0.03, health-medicine 0.01, institutions 0.01: residual allocation for Emerson's publicly described non-climate work in immigration reform, health equity, local civic work, and journalism/institutional capacity. AP describes Emerson as focusing on education, immigration, the environment, and health equity; Forbes' Powell Jobs profile describes Emerson as focused on environmental justice, health, immigration, and education: https://www.forbes.com/profile/laurene-powell-jobs/
