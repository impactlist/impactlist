# Internal Notes

The FTX Future Fund was a project of the FTX Foundation focused on longtermist grantmaking. The June 2022 update reported 262 grants and investments totaling about $132M: about $31M from regranting, $26M from the open call, and $73M staff-led, with about $25M more likely soon. The archived public all-grants page on July 6, 2022 lists 113 public grants/investments totaling about $91.5M.

The category split is a dollar-weighted approximation from that public grant list, not a raw count of areas. The largest tagged area was biorisk/pandemic work, including HelixNano ($10M), AVECRIS ($3.6M), Sherlock Biosciences ($2M), SecureDNA ($1.2M), and other biosecurity projects. Effective-altruism infrastructure maps to meta-theory; AI alignment/safety grants map to ai-risk; exceptional-people fellowships map to education; epistemic-institution grants such as Manifold, forecasting, and public-editor work split between decision-making and institutions. Untagged large grants were manually mapped: Longview ($15M) follows the existing Longview Philanthropy split, Legal Priorities Project maps to global-priorities, ALERT to pandemics, Berkeley Existential Risk Initiative mostly to ai-risk, and the CSER/Legal Priorities book grant to global-priorities. Sources: Future Fund June 2022 update (https://web.archive.org/web/20220701005254/https://ftxfuturefund.org/future-fund-june-2022-update/) and all-grants archive (https://web.archive.org/web/20220706065636/https://ftxfuturefund.org/all-grants/).
