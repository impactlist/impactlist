# Internal Notes

This recipient models contributions into Fundación Amancio Ortega, not the foundation's downstream
grants as separate Amancio Ortega donation rows.

The split is a rounded lifetime/current-portfolio estimate from Fundación Amancio Ortega's official
figures and project pages. The foundation reports 2020-2024 executed social investment of €561.1
million, with €517.7 million in social welfare and €41.6 million in education, plus 2025-2029
commitments of €541.8 million, with €531.3 million in social welfare and €10.5 million in education:
https://www.faortega.org/es/institucion/cifras/

- health-medicine 0.72: the dominant portfolio is rich-country medical infrastructure and care:
  public oncology support (€310M), Spanish proton therapy (€280M), Portuguese proton therapy (€80M),
  Únicas Sant Joan de Déu rare-disease centre (€60M), COVID-19 medical equipment (€47.5M),
  pediatric palliative care (€30M), NIPACE neurological rehabilitation (€19M), the National Hospital
  for Paraplegics in Toledo (€11.24M), and part of the elderly-care-centre programme.
- local-community 0.10: nonclinical social-welfare and community infrastructure, including the
  residual elderly-care/social-service component, food-bank infrastructure, Red Cross elder-loneliness
  work, disability residential facilities, Padre Rubinos/Proxecto Home facilities, and other local
  welfare projects.
- education 0.07: the scholarship programme (€10M annual investment), MPXA journalism/audiovisual
  master's scholarships, iMpulsa, Advanced Talent Training, teacher-training, and related education
  projects.
- disaster-relief 0.07: the €100M DANA aid programme and the emergency-response aspect of the
  COVID-19 programme.
- housing 0.03: the €15M 2023 Cáritas housing assistance programme plus the housing/basic-services
  share of the fulfilled €40M Cáritas Spain programme.
- global-development 0.01: small international poverty/agriculture work, especially the €1.4M
  Southern Highlands of Tanzania farming-techniques project.
