# Internal Notes

This recipient covers the whole Fundación Carlos Slim complex, including its health arm, the
Instituto Carlos Slim de la Salud (endowed 2007). Fundación Telmex Telcel is a separate,
corporate-funded vehicle and is deliberately NOT part of this recipient.

Fractions are a dollar-weighted estimate of the lifetime program mix, anchored on the
foundation's documented large commitments (no public 990-equivalent exists for Mexican
foundations):

- global-health 0.30: $100M to the Global Polio Eradication Initiative (2013, Gates Foundation
  press release); $50M to Salud Mesoamérica 2015 alongside Gates and Spain via the IDB (2010);
  $100M for ~50,000 cataract surgeries in Peru (Forbes 2011); maternal/child health and
  vaccination programs for poor populations across Mexico and Central America.
- education 0.18: aprende.org and Capacítate digital platforms, PruébaT, Khan Academy en
  español and Coursera partnerships, student scholarships and equipment.
- health-medicine 0.15: $65M (2010) + $74M (2013) to the Broad Institute for the SIGMA
  genomic-medicine initiative; ~1M subsidized surgeries and hospital equipment in Mexico.
- global-development 0.12: Capacítate para el Empleo job training across Latin America, $25M
  with the Gates Foundation for CIMMYT agricultural research (2013), $20M Haiti SME fund with
  Clinton/Giustra (2010), microcredit programs.
- arts-culture 0.12: built and operates the free-admission Museo Soumaya (opened 2011,
  ~66,000-piece collection), Mexico City Centro Histórico restoration, Centro de Estudios de
  Historia de México.
- disaster-relief 0.08: ~$105M (MXN 1,978M) for 2017 Mexico earthquake reconstruction;
  MXN 1,000M COVID-19 response (2020); recurring hurricane/flood relief.
- environmental 0.05: $100M WWF–Fundación Carlos Slim conservation alliance (2009), at the
  time the largest private conservation commitment in Mexico.
