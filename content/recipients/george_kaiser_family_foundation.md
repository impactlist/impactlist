# Internal Notes

Place-based anti-poverty foundation (EIN 73-1574370, ~$5.1B assets at end of FY2024), a supporting organization of the Tulsa Community Foundation; nearly all spending targets Tulsa. Fractions blend the FY2024 Form 990 Schedule I grant list ($83.8M to 399 grantees, parsed from the IRS e-file XML) with the lifetime megaprojects that grants alone understate:

- education 0.35: early childhood is the flagship program (Birth through Eight Strategy for Tulsa). FY2024: Community Action Project of Tulsa $20.4M, Tulsa Educare, Start Early, charter and public school support - roughly 38% of the grant list; GKFF also built the Tulsa Educare centers ($18M+) and funds them as a direct activity.
- local-community 0.25: "City Vibrancy" and "Strong Neighborhoods" pillars plus the Tulsa safety-net social services portfolio. Includes the Gathering Place park ($465M with partners, the largest park gift in US history), Tulsa Remote, Tulsa Innovation Labs ($50M), 36 Degrees North, InvestNorth/Kendall-Whittier/East Tulsa revitalization, food banks and day centers.
- health-medicine 0.15: maternal and child health (Healthy Steps, Family Connects, doulas), mental-health and clinic grants (~15% of FY2024 grants), plus $112M to establish and expand the OU School of Community Medicine in Tulsa (2008, 2021).
- arts-culture 0.1: Bob Dylan Center and archive ($20M), Woody Guthrie Center, OKPOP ($5M), Tulsa Artist Fellowship, and steady museum/performing-arts grants (~3% of FY2024 grants but a much larger share of historical direct spending).
- human-rights 0.08: criminal-justice reform - Women in Recovery, Still She Rises, public-defender and commutation projects, reentry programs, ACLU OK (~9% of FY2024 grants; precedent: navigation_fund.md maps criminal justice to human-rights).
- housing 0.07: Choice Neighborhoods Comanche Park ($10.75M FY2024), Housing Partners of Tulsa, Flat Rock Creek Terrace, Habitat, 550 mixed-income units built in Kendall Whittier.

Sources: FY2024 990 Schedule I (IRS e-file XML object 202523219349308302), gkff.org/what-we-do, Wikipedia (George Kaiser Family Foundation).
