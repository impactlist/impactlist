# Internal Notes

Private foundation EIN 04-6856456. The split is based on the 2023 and 2024 IRS XML grant-purpose schedules, using grantmaking only to classify contributions into the foundation. In 2023, the main grant-purpose buckets were climate science and policy research ($22.9M), natural resource conservation/protection ($10.8M), food and agriculture research ($7.6M), Grantham Institute/Centre/Divecha climate research support ($6.9M combined), climate education/communications/finance/action ($8.7M), and smaller environmental-health, marine, conservation, community, and vaccine-research grants. In 2024, the pattern was similar: climate science and policy research ($32.1M), natural resource conservation/protection ($11.0M), food and agriculture research ($6.8M), Grantham Institute/Centre support ($6.7M), climate education/communications/finance ($6.5M), and smaller environmental-health, marine, critical-minerals, environmental-education, and conservation grants. Climate-change receives the majority for climate science, policy, communications, finance, and climate-focused university institutes; environmental covers natural-resource, marine, environmental-health, and conservation work; science-tech covers agricultural, critical-minerals, and applied research components; education covers the education-oriented grant purposes.

The 2023 and 2024 contribution rows themselves are not recorded as Jeremy Grantham donations because Schedule B identifies major non-Grantham contributors: 2023 was $35.0M from Quadrature Climate Fund plus $300K from Arjun Divecha, and 2024 was $27.5M from Quadrature Climate Foundation plus $300K from Arjun Divecha, $1.0M from Grayvest Foundation, and $5K from Cushman Foundation. Source: IRS XML objects 202433199349104768 and 202513219349105041, surfaced through ProPublica/IRS.
