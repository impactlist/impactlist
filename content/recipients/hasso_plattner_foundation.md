# Internal Notes

Split is based on the Hasso Plattner Foundation's public project portfolio rather than a published dollar-by-program grant table, which I could not find. The Foundation says it supports more than 50 projects in 20 countries and more than EUR 455M of philanthropic initiatives over the prior five years, excluding art purchases, through three main focus areas: Science + Education, Social + Conservation, and Art + Culture. Source: https://www.plattnerfoundation.org/en/

- education 0.35: Hasso Plattner Institute, the HPI/University of Potsdam Digital Engineering Faculty, HPI d-school, HP d-school Afrika, the planned Brauhausberg and Griebnitzsee campuses, and education-centered youth programs.
- science-tech 0.20: digital engineering, AI, HPI research clusters, MIT/HPI collaborations, Data4Life as research infrastructure, and related technology programs.
- arts-culture 0.15: Museum Barberini, DAS MINSK, Wildenstein Plattner Institute, Navigating.art, TALES, and public art projects.
- environmental 0.15: SPAC conservation research and EduConservation, Leadership for Conservation in Africa, Dzanga Forest Elephant Project, and Congo Basin rainforest/biodiversity work.
- health-medicine 0.10: Digital Health Cluster, Hasso Plattner Institute for Digital Health at Mount Sinai, Data4Life health-data work, and Embark in Knowledge health services.
- global-development 0.05: child welfare, early-childhood, youth, and community programs in South Africa, Republic of Congo, Namibia, Rwanda, Gabon, Morocco, Senegal, and Ukraine-refugee integration work.

Key supporting pages: HPF endowment (https://www.plattnerfoundation.org/en/endowment), HPI (https://www.plattnerfoundation.org/en/projects/hpi), Digital Health (https://www.plattnerfoundation.org/en/projects/digital-health), Design Thinking (https://www.plattnerfoundation.org/en/projects/design-thinking), SPAC (https://www.plattnerfoundation.org/en/projects/sabine-plattner-african-charities), and Museum Barberini (https://www.plattnerfoundation.org/en/projects/museum-barberini).
