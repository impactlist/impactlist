The largest climate-change grants went to the Institute for Governance and Sustainable Development ($3.78M), Rocky Mountain Institute ($3.45M), Hour Glass Climate ($3.10M), Windward Fund ($3.00M), Carbon180 ($1.98M), League of Conservation Voters Education Fund ($2.00M), Ocean Vision ($2.00M), Spark Climate Solutions ($1.00M), Society for the Protection of Underground Networks ($1.00M), Earthjustice ($0.90M), Third Act Educational Fund ($0.60M), Inside Climate News ($0.50M), and Center for International Environmental Law ($0.25M). Environmental grants included The Nature Conservancy ($3.70M), World Wildlife Fund ($3.48M), Rare ($0.52M including noncash), Rainforest Action Network, Conservation Law Foundation, Alaska Community Action on Toxics, Environmental Working Group, Environmental Health Sciences, and Environmental Law Institute. Health in Harmony and One Acre Fund drive the small global-development share, though their work also has environmental/climate dimensions.

Schedule B contributor names are restricted on the public Form 990 XML. The trust is named for and governed by Jeremy and Hannelore Grantham; Jeremy and Hannelore are listed as trustees, and Schedule O reports trustee-paid expenses and donated services. No 2023 or 2024 Grantham Foundation grant to the trust was found in the foundation grant schedules, reducing the risk that trust inflows are already counted as foundation outflows.
