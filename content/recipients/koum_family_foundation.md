# Internal Notes

The split is a rounded allocation from the Koum Family Foundation's recent 990-PF grant pattern, especially the FY2024 grant list in ProPublica object 202513179349103856. The largest 2024 grants were Jewish and Israel-focused community, education, health, religious, aid, and advocacy/civic organizations: European Jewish Association ($11.0M across two rows), Federation of Jewish Communities of the CIS and named local FJC projects (roughly $8.6M), Oshman Family Jewish Community Center ($2.5M), Israel Healthcare Foundation ($3.9M across three rows), American Friends of Sheba Medical Center ($2.0M), Hadassah ($2.0M), Yad Eliezer ($2.0M), BBYO ($1.6M), iTrek ($3.0M), Birthright Israel Foundation ($2.0M), Israel on Campus Coalition ($1.35M), and multiple Jewish schools, camps, community centers, universities, medical charities, and pro-Israel civic/advocacy nonprofits. Existing precedent treats "Multiple Jewish Community Organizations" as local-community, while medical-center rows map to health-medicine and university/school/youth-learning rows map to education.

The FY2024 filing shows $104.1M of grants paid and $93.2M of charitable disbursements, but those grants are not recorded as Jan Koum donation rows because the donor-row event is the contribution into this foundation.
