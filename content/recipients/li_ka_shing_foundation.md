# Internal Notes

Covers Li Ka-shing's foundation group: the Li Ka Shing Foundation (Hong Kong, est. 1980), the Li Ka Shing (Canada) Foundation (Toronto, est. 2005), and the investment-holding Li Ka Shing (Global) Foundation — one umbrella in practice (single website/brand, jointly announced funding). LKSF accepts no external funds; everything it grants is Li's money.

Fractions approximate the share of its >HK$30 billion cumulative grantmaking, 1980–2025, ~80% in mainland China and Hong Kong ([LKSF timeline](https://www.lksf.org/timeline/)). Anchors:

- **education 0.43** — Shantou University, the foundation's self-described cornerstone, has received over HK$12B (40% of all grants) including its medical college ([LKSF](https://www.lksf.org/initiative-project/shantou-university/)); ~3/4 of that is attributed here and the medical college/affiliated-hospitals share to health-medicine. Plus US$130M to Technion to create Guangdong Technion Israel Institute of Technology (2013), HK$500M to HKUST (2019), S$100M to the Lee Kuan Yew School of Public Policy (2007), HK$100M to PolyU (2001), US$11.5M to Singapore Management University's library (2002), CKGSB, Cheung Kong Scholars Programme, and mainland rural/distance education programs.
- **health-medicine 0.22** — Shantou University Medical College and affiliated hospitals (~1/4 of the STU total); HK$1B to HKU's Faculty of Medicine (2005); a worldwide string of medical-school/institute gifts (UC Berkeley US$40M 2005 + US$10M 2014, Stanford US$37M, Oxford Big Data Institute £20M 2013, U Alberta virology CA$28M, St. Michael's Hospital Toronto CA$25M+, Cambridge cancer research £7M+, U Manitoba US$2M 2025); Hong Kong hospice (HK$82M+), Love Can Help patient assistance, 2023 AI medical-equipment gifts to HKU/CUHK medicine, and the 2025–26 histotripsy liver-cancer machines and subsidy program (incl. S$12M jointly with Temasek Trust).
- **religious 0.11** — Tsz Shan Monastery alone received more than HK$3.1B for land, construction and operations (10% of all grants, [LKSF](https://www.lksf.org/timeline/)), plus Buddhist-studies and temple support.
- **global-health 0.09** — "Heart of Gold" National Hospice Service Programme in mainland China, >RMB1B since 1998 across 29 provinces; Brightness Action free cataract surgeries; New Milestone prosthetics for rural amputees; other charitable medical delivery in developing-region China.
- **local-community 0.07** — HK$1B "Crunch Time Instant Relief Fund" for Hong Kong SMEs and workers (2019–20); ongoing Hong Kong elderly, youth and community programs; Shantou local welfare.
- **disaster-relief 0.05** — Sichuan earthquake (~HK$130M, 2008), COVID-19 relief incl. HK$100M for Wuhan medical staff (2020), Henan floods (HK$20M, 2021), Tai Po Wang Fuk Court fire (HK$80M, 2025), Indian Ocean tsunami and earlier flood appeals.
- **global-development 0.03** — the foundation's anti-poverty programs outside health/education (rural livelihoods, poverty alleviation in western China).

Donor inflow rows from Li Ka-shing are floored at this same HK$30B cumulative figure (see `content/donations/li_ka_shing.md`), so fractions here are what convert his contributions into modeled impact.
