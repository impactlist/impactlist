# Internal Notes

Category split is based primarily on the FY2024 Form 990-PF XML grant schedule, which listed $227.5M in charitable disbursements and $538.6M in contributions received, plus public descriptions of the Marcus Foundation as focused on medical research, Jewish causes, free enterprise/civic institutions, veterans, children, and local community. Largest FY2024 grants included Shepherd Center Foundation ($17.6M), Emory University ($14.2M), Children's Healthcare of Atlanta Foundation ($9.4M), Boca Raton Regional Hospital Foundation ($6.75M), MD Anderson ($6.18M), Thomas Jefferson University Hospital ($6.14M), and Emory Medical Care Foundation ($3.5M), supporting a large health-medicine allocation. Large Jewish/religious and Israel/Jewish identity grants included Hillel ($10.6M), Nefesh B'Nefesh ($5.9M), Marcus Jewish Community Center of Atlanta ($5.7M), Jewish Education Project ($5.1M), IDI Foundation ($3.8M), Foundation for Jewish Camp ($3.1M), Israel on Campus Coalition ($2.2M), Birthright Israel ($2.1M), iTrek ($2M), and many smaller Jewish-community organizations. Education includes Weber School, KIPP Metro Atlanta, Florida Education Foundation, Georgia Tech, charter-school groups, Junior Achievement, and university foundations where the grant was not clearly medical. Institutions covers free-enterprise/civic-policy and national-security think tanks such as Foundation for Defense of Democracies, PragerU, Job Creators Network Foundation, Institute for Humane Studies, and similar 501(c)(3) recipients. Local-community covers veterans, public safety, human services, and Atlanta-area community grants; arts-culture covers Woodruff Arts, High Museum, museums, zoo/aquarium-type recipients; science-tech covers research alliance and science institutions not already classified as medical.
