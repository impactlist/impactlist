# Internal Notes

Category split is based primarily on the FY2023 and FY2024 IRS XML grant schedules for EIN 82-1927087, with ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer used to identify filings and contribution/grant totals. The FY2023 schedule listed $22.84M in grants, including $18.96M to Milken Institute, $1.16M to Prostate Cancer Foundation, school/education grants, arts grants, and local/community grants. The FY2024 schedule listed $26.07M in grants, dominated by a $20.0M grant to Jewish Community Foundation Charitable Fund, plus Milken Institute/Milken Scholars, Milken Community School, George Washington University, Prostate Cancer Foundation, Mattel Children's Hospital at UCLA, arts organizations, and local/community charities.

`local-community` receives the largest share because the FY2024 schedule is dominated by the Jewish Community Foundation charitable-fund transfer and many smaller community, Jewish communal, public-safety, youth, and human-service grants. `institutions` captures the large Milken Institute grants, treated as policy/economic-institution work rather than direct school delivery. `education` covers Milken Scholars, K-12 schools, higher-education, and youth education rows. `health-medicine` covers Prostate Cancer Foundation, hospitals, disease research, and health-care rows. `arts-culture` covers Los Angeles Ballet, opera, philharmonic, museums, and related arts/culture rows. `other` is a small residual for opaque or mixed-purpose rows.

Sources: ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer for EIN 82-1927087; IRS XML object 202412899349101556 for FY2023; IRS XML object 202522889349101362 for FY2024.
