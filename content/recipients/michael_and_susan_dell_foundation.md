# Internal Notes

Category split is based primarily on the foundation's recent 990-PF grant schedule and program footprint. The FY2024 IRS XML for object 202543189349104239 lists $163.8M of charitable disbursements and 836 grant rows. The largest grant was $50.0M to Charter School Growth Fund for the Dell Catalyst Fund, and other major rows included UT Austin/Dell Scholars college-completion programs, Mobile Loaves & Fishes/Community First! Village housing, Jewish community grants, and a broad set of India, South Africa, and US education/economic-mobility programs.

Education receives the largest share because the 2024 grant schedule is dominated by K-12, charter-school growth, college access/completion, Dell Scholars, skilling, and education technology. Global development captures India/South Africa economic mobility, livelihoods, financial inclusion, and poverty-alleviation work that is not purely health or school delivery. Health-medicine covers US medical, mental-health, pediatric, and local health-system grants. Global-health covers international cleft, fistula, trachoma, vision, and surgery grants. Housing is separated for Community First! Village and related homelessness work. Local-community captures residual Austin, Jewish community, and place-based community grants.

Sources checked include ProPublica Nonprofit Explorer for EIN 36-4336415, official IRS XML for FY2024, and public foundation descriptions noting work in the United States, India, Israel, and South Africa.
