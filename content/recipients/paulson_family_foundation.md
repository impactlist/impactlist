# Internal Notes

Split based on parsed Paulson Family Foundation grant rows from ProPublica rendered 990-PF filings for FY2013-FY2024
($597.6M total grants parsed). The largest grant stream is Harvard/SEAS-related: $356.25M to President and Fellows of
Harvard College/RSO accounts, treated as science-tech because the public gift named Harvard's School of Engineering
and Applied Sciences. NYU, Success Academy, schools, and Hebrew University scholarship/facilities grants drive the
education share. Central Park Conservancy, UJA, local hospitals, and local civic/social-service grants drive
local-community. Tel Aviv Museum, School of American Ballet, NYC Ballet, preservation, and museum/cultural grants
drive arts-culture. Manhattan Institute, Council on Foreign Relations, Hoover Institution, Milken Center, and similar
think-tank/civic grants drive institutions. Benefactors of Ecuador is treated as global-health because it funded a
children's hospital in Guayaquil. Cancer, depression, and hospital research grants drive health-medicine. Opaque
transfers such as J & J Independent Foundation and miscellaneous general charitable rows are left in other.

Primary source for filings: https://projects.propublica.org/nonprofits/organizations/263922995. Full rendered filing
examples used for the grant list include FY2024 object 202503229349101015 and FY2017 object 201833199349104433.
