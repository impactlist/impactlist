# Internal Notes

This split is an approximate portfolio model for contributions into Charles and Lynn Schusterman Family Philanthropies, not a reconstruction of downstream grants as donor rows. The organization says it invested $4B+ across its portfolios since 1987 and funded $470M to organizations in 2025. Its current work page lists seven portfolios: Criminal Justice, Democracy and Voting Rights, Education, Gender and Reproductive Equity, Hometown of Tulsa, Jewish Community, and Funding in Israel. The 2024 IRS XML for EIN 73-1312965 reports $333,457,955 of grants paid, but the public XML collapses grant detail into "See Attached Statement", so the split uses the foundation's current stated portfolio mix.

Mapping: education covers the education portfolio plus Israel/Jewish education work; social-justice covers criminal justice, economic equity, and gender-equity systems change; human-rights covers reproductive freedom, LGBTQ inclusion, child/family safety, anti-hate, and underserved Israeli communities; institutions covers democracy/voting rights and public-policy work in the U.S. and Israel; local-community covers Tulsa, Jewish community infrastructure, and Israeli social-service work; conflict-mitigation covers the smaller Israel/regional-stability and peace component; religious is a small allocation for Jewish community/religious-life work. Sources: https://www.schusterman.org/who-we-are, https://www.schusterman.org/what-we-do, https://www.schusterman.org/what-we-do/funding-in-the-us, https://www.schusterman.org/what-we-do/funding-in-israel.
