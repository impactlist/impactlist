# Internal Notes

The split is based on the Parker Foundation's stated focus areas and the dominant public grant pattern: life sciences, global public health, civic engagement, and art. The largest documented downstream commitment is the Parker Institute for Cancer Immunotherapy, publicly described as a USD 250 million Parker/Parker Foundation cancer immunotherapy initiative, which drives the high health-medicine share. Other named medical grants include the Sean N. Parker Center for Allergy and Asthma Research at Stanford, UCSF autoimmune research, Stand Up To Cancer / Cancer Research Institute, and cancer-immunotherapy network work. These are downstream grants/initiatives of the Parker Foundation or Parker Institute, so they inform this recipient allocation but are not separate Sean Parker donation rows once the money has been counted entering the foundation. Global-health share comes primarily from the UCSF Malaria Elimination Initiative and related global public health work. Institutions covers civic engagement and economic-policy grants such as Economic Innovation Group / New Venture Fund and Code for America type work. Arts-culture covers smaller visible museum/art grants in recent 990-PF grant lists, including Museum Associates and MOCA Los Angeles.
