# Internal Notes

The Simons Foundation (EIN 13-3794889, founded 1994 by Jim and Marilyn Simons) funds basic research in mathematics and the sciences. Fractions approximate the share of program dollars across its divisions, grounded in its CY2024 990-PF grant list ($272M of external grants), annual-report division structure, and historical grantmaking:

- science-tech 0.70: Mathematics & Physical Sciences (Simons Center for Geometry and Physics at Stony Brook, Simons Institute for the Theory of Computing at Berkeley, IHES, MIT math, arXiv support), Life Sciences (New York Genome Center — single largest 2024 grant at $20M, quantitative/marine biology, origins of life), neuroscience (Simons Collaboration on the Global Brain), and the in-house Flatiron Institute (~$130M/yr of internal computational-science spending across astrophysics, biology, quantum, math, and neuroscience centers).
- health-medicine 0.20: the Simons Foundation Autism Research Initiative (SFARI), historically on the order of $75-90M/yr, plus clinical research vehicles (Clinical Research Associates) and medically directed grants (e.g. $5.1M University of Edinburgh clinical autism research in 2024, Stony Brook medical-school support within the 2011 $150M gift).
- education 0.10: Math for America (long the foundation's flagship education grantee at ~$25M/yr), Science Sandbox/outreach, CUNY astrophysics bridge program, STEM-diversity programs at Stony Brook.

Large university gifts (Stony Brook $500M endowment 2023, CUNY $75M 2024 with SFI) are paid out of this portfolio and are reflected in the weighting rather than recorded as donor rows.
