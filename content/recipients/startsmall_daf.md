# Internal Notes

This recipient represents Jack Dorsey's #startsmall donor-advised-fund giving. The donation row records the public Details-tab aggregate, not individual grants out: https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1-eGxq2mMoEGwgSpNVL5j2sa6ToojZUZ-Zun8h2oBAR4/edit#gid=17278742

The category fractions use the public Donations tab (387 rows, $634,681,561 disbursed through the 2023-12-19 latest row) as the grantmaking portfolio for the DAF aggregate:

- pandemics 0.40: COVID-19 response and epidemic-preparedness grants dominate the ledger, led by Resolve to Save Lives / Vital Strategies ($88M), CORE testing and response ($30M), Indiana University AEGIS ($10M), CARE India COVID-19 relief ($10M), and many smaller COVID response, PPE, tracing, testing, and relief rows.
- education 0.12: girls' education and school access grants such as Oakland Public Education Fund ($10M), Malala Fund ($5M), Khan Academy ($5M), Girls Who Code ($2M), Teach For All, CAMFED, Girl Scouts of Northern California, and many local youth education rows.
- social-justice 0.12: racial justice, criminal-justice reform, and civic-equity grants including Boston University's Center for Antiracist Research ($10M), FUSE Corps ($7.6M), REFORM Alliance, Council on Criminal Justice, Know Your Rights Camp, Oregon Justice Resource Center, FREEAMERICA, Black feminist and Black-led organizing groups.
- global-development 0.10: cash-transfer and poverty-relief work, including the Open Research basic-income project ($15M), Mayors for Guaranteed Income / Reinvent Stockton ($18M+), GiveDirectly / Project 100 ($10M), Humanity Forward Foundation cash-transfer work, Water.org, and international girls' economic-mobility programs.
- climate-change 0.09: Clara Lionel Foundation climate resilience and climate justice grants ($45M), plus smaller climate-justice, Indigenous environmental, SUGi urban-forest, and related resilience rows.
- science-tech 0.06: open-source, privacy, internet-infrastructure, and public-interest technology grants including OpenSats ($10M), Freedom of the Press Foundation ($11M), teamopen.tech ($5M, treated as public-interest tech for the split but not itemized as a donor row), Bitcoin Legal Defense Fund, Tor, Signal, Internet Archive, Open Web Advocacy, Calyx, GrapheneOS, and Brink.
- disaster-relief 0.04: World Central Kitchen, Maui wildfire relief, Ukraine humanitarian relief, Texas winter-storm relief, food banks, and other non-pandemic emergency response.
- health-medicine 0.03: reproductive health, mental health, domestic-violence health services, medical research, UCSF programs, MSI, and related health rows not classified as pandemic response.
- human-rights 0.02: refugee, LGBTIQ, anti-trafficking, gender-based-violence, and rights-defense rows.
- local-community 0.01 and arts-culture 0.01: residual place-based community, housing, family-services, arts, cultural-center, theater, film, and museum rows.

Rows or candidates not used as standalone donor rows under the record-money-once policy include all downstream DAF grants in the Donations tab. Rows treated cautiously or excluded from category evidence where appropriate include Center for Popular Democracy Action Fund ($125K, 501(c)(4)-style advocacy), Humanity Forward's 2021 advocacy/Congress row ($2M), Mayvenn's for-profit-associated salon GoFundMe row ($500K), and direct political campaign contributions outside #startsmall. These are small relative to the aggregate and do not materially affect the rounded split.
